The best way to learn a certain programming language is by developing your own program with it. Particularlay, if the written program is closely related to your ongoing work (at your job), it goes without saying that this pratice will help not only to develop your programming skill but to solve the matters at your own hand along the career path. [Rust][official-rust-website] is one of the low-level, high-performance programming langauge I'm eager to learn but hesitant to start because of its complexity and steep learning curve ([as they say][hengli-on-rust]). Better late than never. Let's just start building one already. I'll first begin with developing a simple Rust code to count the occurrences of trinucleotide from fasta file.

(Of note, in this series of practice sessions [Rust by Developing], I'm hoping to build one swiss-army-knife type of Rust-based program adding bit by bit as I go along)
